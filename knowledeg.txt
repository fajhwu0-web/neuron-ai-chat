A bank is a financial institution that is licensed to receive deposits and provide loans to individuals, businesses, and governments. Banks play a major role in the financial system because they help people store money safely, transfer funds, and access credit.

Banks offer many services to customers. One of the most common services is accepting deposits. When people deposit money into a bank account, the bank keeps the money safe and allows the customer to withdraw it later.

Another important service provided by banks is lending money. Banks provide loans to people and companies that need money for various purposes such as buying a house, starting a business, or paying for education. The borrower must repay the loan with interest over time.

Interest is the extra money that a borrower pays to the bank for using its funds. The interest rate determines how much additional money the borrower must pay back.

Banks also provide checking accounts and savings accounts. A checking account is typically used for everyday transactions such as paying bills and making purchases. A savings account is designed for storing money and earning interest over time.

Banks also issue credit cards and debit cards. A debit card allows customers to spend money directly from their bank account. A credit card allows customers to borrow money from the bank for purchases and pay it back later.

Modern banks provide online banking services. Online banking allows customers to check their balances, transfer money, pay bills, and manage their accounts through the internet.

Banks also help facilitate payments between individuals and businesses. They manage electronic transfers, international payments, and financial transactions between different institutions.

Central banks are special banks that control a country's monetary policy. A central bank regulates the money supply, controls interest rates, and ensures the stability of the financial system.

Commercial banks are the most common type of banks that serve individuals and businesses. Investment banks focus on helping companies raise capital and manage investments.

Banks are essential for economic growth because they support businesses, provide credit, and enable financial transactions across the economy.
